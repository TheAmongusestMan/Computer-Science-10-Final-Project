Currently in high school and working torwards succeeding within the IBDP diploma program offered by
my school, this website is to present a grade-10 Computer Science final project whereby I aim to
create a working two-player 2d combat game with animations, a life system, and working attacks.

Within this project I aim to understand the basics of the video game engine Unity, including
it's assets, tools, and the programing language associated with the development process of a game
created utilising it' engine. The final product will be a two-player duel or battle-esqe game, with
two characters, a health and attack system, as well as uniqe enviroment sprites alongside
movement and attack animations.

The results I present as my final product is the third/culmination of three program's worth of projects
as the first two I utilised more as testbeds or sandboxes to learn the basics of the engine and more or less
as a sheet of "Scrap paper" to make mistakes upon as the tools within the Unity engine are complex and
often overlap with eachother. My final thoughts upon the Unity engine is one of anticipation as despite the
complexity in code and format of my simple game, I belive it still to be lacking in features and functions which
perhaps I could include in a later date.
